Model Summary

Problem

Undiagnosed diabetes is uncommon in the analytic cohort, so a default 0.50 classification threshold misses too many cases. The project reframes the model as a screening tool rather than a diagnostic classifier.

Decision Rule

The baseline logistic regression predicts risk from non-lab variables. If predicted risk is at least 0.03, the app recommends HbA1c follow-up.

What’s Included

  • Target definition: HbA1c >= 6.5%, no self-reported diagnosis, and not taking diabetes medication.
  • Feature engineering: analytic cohort plus non-laboratory screening predictors.
  • Modeling: logistic regression baseline compared with simple clinical rules and tree-based alternatives.
  • Threshold selection: 0.03 chosen to prioritize sensitivity for screening follow-up.
  • Shiny app: local R app in the GitHub repo with the same inputs and recommendation language.
